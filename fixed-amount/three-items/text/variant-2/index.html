One on top of the other on small screens

This is a pretty basic way to display three items: on a small screen we stack them, on wide screens we place them next to each other. The hard part here is finding a logical breakpoint to switch from one to three columns without breaking the layout on any resolution.

One to three, when to break?

If the ideal measure is between 45 and 75 characters like Brinkhurst says, we have a problem. There's no way to make the step from one to three columns within this theory. Adding white-space left and right until three columns fit might clash with the rest of the items on the page.

First idea

This the first version of this idea. It grows until the single column is 75 characters wide. It then collects white-space until there's room for three columns of 45 characters. The amount of characters is calculated with whis tool.
